FForm Calculator 1.0a

Concept and Development by Peter Hofmann, october 2018.
Calculation algorithms based on demonstrations in the book uFORM iFORM by Ralf Peyn, 2017 and the Laws of Form by George Spencer-Brown, 1969–2015.

Calculate FORMs with 4 values (…) either as constants (0: unmarked, 1: marked, 2: undetermined, 3: imaginary) or variables (single characters: a,b,c,… or labels, using quotes: "Ball", "The Earth", "3×2=12", …). When using variables, all possible interpretations will be computed.

Self-equivalent re-entry forms with nested arguments (as described in the book uFORM iFORM by Ralf Peyn) can also be processed by using curly brackets {…} to mark the re-entry form and separating the nested variables/constants/forms with a comma: {a,b,…}. Using pipes before the arguments, you can also specify open forms (without the outer cross) {open|a,…} and either even {2r|a,…} or odd {2r+1|a,…} re-entry numbers as well as combinations {2r|open|a,…}.

Enter a formula in parenthesis notation (see examples below) and click "calculate" to calculate the whole FORM and "view JSON" to display a JSON-representation of the FORM that the calculator is working with. In this representation, forms with the property "unmarked": true behave like groups and will not be displayed or computed.

Notation examples:

  • form: (), (a), (0), (2)3, (ab), ((a)b), ((a)b)c, (a)(b), a(23("Ball")"Socks")
  • re-entry form (nested variables): {a},{a,b}, {(a)2b,c}
  • re-entry form with even re-entry number: {2r|a,b,c}
  • re-entry form with odd re-entry number: {2r+1|a,b,c}
  • re-entry form without outer cross: {2r|open|a,b,c}, {2r+1|open|a,b,c}
